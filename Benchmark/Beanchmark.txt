This comprehensive writeup summarizes the series of **KBench (Kernel Benchmark)** tasks executed during this session. The evaluations were designed to push the boundaries of Large Language Model (LLM) performance across five core cognitive domains: **Retrieval, Logic/Reasoning, Social Cognition, Executive Function,** and **Metacognition.**

---

## 1. Retrieval & Attention: The "Needle in a Haystack"
These tests evaluated the models' ability to isolate specific data points within massive volumes of noise.
* **Tasks:** Finding a project codename in repetitive prose and extracting non-numeric strings from dense CSV structures.
* **Key Findings:** While most models showed perfect retrieval in prose, structured data (CSV) proved more challenging. **GPT-5.4** and **Gemini 2.5 Pro** remained perfect, but **DeepSeek-R1** struggled significantly with CSV extraction, suggesting that "reasoning" models can sometimes over-analyze simple structural data.

## 2. Symbolic & In-Context Logic
These tasks required models to adopt entirely new, fictional logical systems provided within a single prompt.
* **Tasks:** The **"Prime-Swap"** system (replacing prime/even numbers with specific words) and **"Reverse Logic Arithmetic"** (where `+` is subtraction and `-` is multiplication).
* **Key Findings:** Most models exhibited high "plasticity," successfully overriding their training. However, **DeepSeek-R1** showed a "habituation trap," where its strong internal training in standard mathematics occasionally overrode the fictional rules it was instructed to follow.

## 3. Social Cognition & Theory of Mind (ToM)
These benchmarks measured the AI’s "Social IQ" its ability to track the conflicting beliefs and intentions of multiple characters.
* **Tasks:** Standard false-belief tests and multi-scenario recursive reasoning (e.g., "A knows that B thinks C is lying").
* **Key Findings:** Basic social reasoning is now a "solved" baseline. However, in **Multi-Scenario** tests, a performance gap emerged: **Gemini 2.5/3** and **Claude Opus 4.6** maintained perfect scores, while **GPT-5.4** and **DeepSeek-R1** dropped to 67% accuracy, struggling with complex, nested social perspectives.

## 4. Executive Function & Constraint Adherence
This domain tested "System 2" thinking the ability to follow multi-step plans and suppress impulsive responses.
* **Tasks:** Solving logic puzzles, following "no-go" constraints (Inhibition), and executing meteorological decision trees with strict priority levels.
* **Key Findings:** This was the most difficult domain. In the **Inhibition Benchmark**, nearly every model (including Claude and Gemini) failed with a **0.00 score**, proving that LLMs still struggle to fight their own probabilistic weights. However, they were excellent at **Conditional Planning**, with **Deepseek V3.1** and **Qwen 3** showing perfect algorithmic follow-through.

## 5. Metacognition & Self-Awareness
These tasks measured the "wisdom" of the AI knowing when it doesn't know an answer or when the data provided is corrupted.
* **Tasks:** Detecting "Unknowable" facts (e.g., Cervantes' breakfast) and flagging corrupted datasets.
* **Key Findings:** **Qwen 3 Next Thinking** was the standout leader in data integrity detection. In "Self-Awareness," **GPT-5.4** led the pack (0.78), showing a higher willingness to admit ignorance compared to **DeepSeek-R1**, which often attempted to "reason" its way into a hallucination.

## Final Performance Summary Table

| Domain | Top Performers | Common Failure Mode |

### Overall Conclusion
The benchmark results reveal a shifting landscape. While **GPT-5.4** and **Claude Opus 4.6** remain the most balanced "all-rounders," **Gemini 2.5/3** has become the leader in social reliability. **Qwen** and **DeepSeek** have mastered algorithmic logic but still struggle with the "human" nuances of metacognition and social perspective taking. The biggest hurdle remains **Inhibition**: models still find it almost impossible to *not* do what they are most frequently trained to do.
